🔗 Correlation Analysis
📖 What is correlation? It measures how closely two assets move together. +1.0 = they move in lockstep. 0 = no relationship. -1.0 = they move opposite. If TAO and BTC are highly correlated, holding both doesn't diversify your risk much — you're doubling down on the same bet.
⛏️ TAO Emission & Supply Schedule
📖 Why supply matters: Like Bitcoin, TAO has a fixed max supply of 21 million tokens and a halving schedule that cuts new emissions every ~4 years. Less new supply hitting the market means existing tokens become scarcer — a potential price catalyst if demand stays constant or grows.
🥩 Staking Yield Calculator
📖 How staking works: You can stake TAO to a validator (earn base TAO yield ~2-4% APY) or to a subnet (earn subnet alpha, often higher APY but with more risk). Subnet staking converts your TAO to "alpha" tokens — if the subnet grows, alpha appreciates on top of the yield. If it shrinks, alpha loses value even with staking rewards.

📊 Backtest Configuration
📖 What is backtesting? Unlike Monte Carlo which simulates random futures, backtesting applies a strategy to actual historical prices. It answers: "If I had done X, what would have happened?" There's only one answer because there's only one history. This tests strategies against reality — not probability.
